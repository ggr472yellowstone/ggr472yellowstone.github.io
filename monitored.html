Monitored Species

Trumpeter Swan - Cygnus buccinator

The Trumpeter Swan is the largest species of swan and has a neck that is the same length as its body! They live in wetlands and have a lifespan of up to 30 years. These birds mate for life and can lay 4 to 5 eggs per clutch. Seeing a Trumpeter Swan is a beautiful sight!
Find more information on the Trumpeter Swan here!

American White Pelican - Pelecanus erythrorhynchos

The American White Pelican is one of the largest North American birds, and is known for its ability to extend its neck to allow for more water and food to enter its mouth. This species are experts at stealing food, so if you have any trail mix on you, beware! These birds forage for their food during the day, but in breeding season they switch to foraging at night.
Find more information on the American White Pelican here!

Peregrine Falcons - Falco peregrinus

The Peregrine Falcon lives on shorelines and preys on smaller birds. These raptors are known to be trained in captivity and are monitored in order to reestablish population. They are found on every continent in the world besides Antarctica. From below, these birds have a white belly with black markings, so they can be easily distinguished in flight.

Ospreys - Pandion Haliaetus

The Osprey is a unique raptor because of its ability to dive into water in order to catch its main source of food, fish. Ospreys make large stick nests and spend most of their time monitoring shorelines and bodies of water for prey. These birds can be identified by their striking yellow eyes and white patches on their head and neck.

Bald Eagles - Haliaeetus leucocephalus

The Bald Eagle is an important figure in America, as well as a spiritual figure for Native Americans, and a powerful presence in YNP. They can make nests that are several feet long, which you can see in tall dead or nearly dead trees. These birds are found nationwide in the US and Canada, and can be seen swooping out of the sky to catch fish in bodies of water.
Find more information on Bald Eagles here!

Common Loon - Gavia immer

The Common Loon has striking features including red eyes, a sharp bill, and a black & white patterned body. This duck is an expert at surface diving underwater to retrieve its favorite food, fish! You can find this species ground nesting near lakes and ponds.
Find more information on the Common Loon here!

Golden Eagle - Aquila chrysaetos

The Golden Eagle is the largest and fastest of the raptors. These birds eat small mammals and keep their nests on cliffs. They are prevalent throughout North America, especially in the West. The Golden Eagle has feathers resemblant of gold when they shine in the sun, so keep your eye out for these fast and beautiful creatures!
