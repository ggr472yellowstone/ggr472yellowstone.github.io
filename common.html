Common Species

Bald Eagle - Haliaeetus leucocephalus

The Bald Eagle is a breeding species in the park. These raptors live over 30 years and mate for life. You can find them in their large nests on top of barren trees near rivers and lakes. The Bald eagle eats fish, small mammals, snakes, and small birds.

Bufflehead Duck - Bucephala Albeola

The Bufflehead duck is a breeding species in Yellowstone. It most commonly lives and breeds by lakes, rivers, and bays in Northwestern America. They are the smallest diving ducks in North America and forage for food underwater and eat insects, snails, and water plants. These ducks are found in small flocks and migrate together towards the coasts as winter rolls in. You might think the name ‘Bufflehead Duck’ sounds funny, but it describes the shape of the duck's large head!

Great Horned Owl - Bubo virginianus

The Great Horned Owl is a breeding species in the park. In fact, this is one of the most common owls in North America. These owls nest in forests and feed on small mammals such as mice. They are ferocious predators that have talons with a deadly grip and the ability to aerial dive onto their prey.

Common Merganser - Mergus merganser

The Common Merganser is a breeding species in the park. These ducks also go by the names ‘sawbills’, ‘fish ducks’, and ‘goosanders’. The Common Merganser is a large duck that eats fish, frogs, and other small animals. Their flocks can include up to 75 birds and they nest in northern forests near lakes and rivers.
